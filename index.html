Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about commercial roofing costs, systems, and maintenance.

Commercial roofing costs range from $4.50 to $16.00 per square foot installed in 2026, depending on the system. TPO averages $5.50–$8.50/sq ft, EPDM $4.50–$7.50/sq ft, PVC $6.50–$10.00/sq ft, modified bitumen $5.00–$8.50/sq ft, standing seam metal $9.00–$16.00/sq ft, and spray foam $5.50–$9.50/sq ft. Projects over 20,000 sq ft often qualify for volume discounts of 10–15%. Labor costs are typically 10–20% higher than residential due to commercial project complexity and safety requirements.

The best commercial roofing system depends on your building's use, local climate, and budget. TPO is the most popular choice (~40% market share) for general commercial buildings due to energy efficiency and cost-effectiveness. PVC is ideal for restaurants and food processing due to chemical and grease resistance. EPDM excels in cold climates with its flexibility. Standing seam metal offers the longest lifespan (40–70 years) and lowest total cost of ownership for buildings with long holding periods. A life-cycle cost analysis comparing 25-year total costs across systems is the best way to evaluate options.

Commercial roof lifespans vary by system: TPO 20–30 years, EPDM 20–30 years, PVC 25–35 years, modified bitumen 15–25 years, built-up roofing (BUR) 20–30 years, spray foam 20–30 years with recoating, and standing seam metal 40–70 years. Proper maintenance through a preventive maintenance program with biannual inspections can extend any system's lifespan by 5–10 years. Per NRCA guidelines, neglecting maintenance is the single largest factor in premature roof failure.

Consider full replacement if your commercial roof has widespread membrane failure, saturated insulation (confirmed by infrared scan), multiple active leaks, or is past 75% of its expected lifespan. Roof coatings can extend life 10–15 years at 50–70% less cost than full replacement if the existing membrane and insulation are still in good condition. Targeted repairs make sense for isolated damage or leaks when the overall system is sound. A professional moisture survey with core samples provides the data needed to make the right decision.

Look for manufacturer NDL (No Dollar Limit) warranties covering both materials and labor for 20–30 years. System warranties from manufacturers like Carlisle, Firestone, GAF, and Sika Sarnafil are stronger than material-only warranties because they cover the entire roofing assembly. Ensure the installing contractor is an authorized applicator for the manufacturer. Warranty compliance typically requires documented maintenance inspections at least twice per year — failure to maintain this schedule is the most common reason warranty claims are denied.
